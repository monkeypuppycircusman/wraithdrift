Wraith Drift — drift purple bleeding into phonk red. The atmosphere is slow drifting fog ellipses in purple and magenta. Waveform gradient sweeps from drift purple through to phonk red. Cursor is a little ghost with two eyes.
The choir visualizer at the top shows formant frequency bars in a purple-to-pink sweep — when anything is playing it comes alive and the tape flutter indicator in the corner tracks the wow/flutter oscillation in real time showing cents of pitch deviation.
The tape warble engine runs on every single sound — two LFOs per voice: a slow wow (0.3–0.8Hz, wide) and a faster flutter (6–12Hz, subtle). The Tape knob scales both simultaneously. Turn it up and everything starts seasick.
12 hold pads — tap once to latch on, tap again to release:
Purple — Choir Aah / Mmm / Ooh — full 5-voice formant synthesis. Each "singer" is slightly detuned with individual vibrato and tape warble. Three vowel shapes: open Aah, closed humming Mmm, round Ooh. The choir label updates to say the choir speaks / hums / calls. Ghost Pad is a high thin dissonant cluster in the overtones.
Red — Phonk Brass (detuned sawtooth through bandpass), Sub Drone (pure 36Hz sine with tape wow). One-shots: Tape Squeal (pitch drops from 3200Hz to 80Hz), 808 Tail (the phonk staple — pitch drops from 160Hz to 42Hz with that long tail).
Blue-grey — Dark Atmos (pink noise low-passed), Rain Tape (rain noise with a wow LFO riding the filter), one-shot Pitch Drop.
Choir knob controls the depth of the vocal formant blend mixed into every keyboard note you play — so you can have the choir buried underneath your chords. Choir selector — Distant / Close / Buried changes the formant tuning and amplitude.
